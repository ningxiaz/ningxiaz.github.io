ECHORUSInstant Conversations on Every Webpage!

Echorus is a side project of mine with two other friends. It is a platform that allows comments on any webpage and provides a social way of great content discovery. I started working on this project as a designer and front-end developer in the fall of 2012 and now it will soon be alpha tested. For reasons of schedules etc., I’m not working on it anymore.

More specifically, you start by adding the “echo” bookmark to your browser. On every webpage, by clicking on the bookmark a sidebar will show up with all the social interactions regarding this page. You can like or dislike this page, and check who has liked or disliked it, as well as view and heart the comments and join the conversation yourself. You can follow your friends or people you’re interested in, and their actions and comments will appear in you feed, where you will discover great content and engage in discussions.

It goes beyond simply adding comment sections on webpages, it adds social value to the web. For example, people can find information of credibility and usefulness regarding the page/article in the comments, which might work better than Google’s PageRank. Now lots of discussions around links are scattered around numerous social networks, whereas places like Facebook are not necessarily a good place for thoughtful discussions. There has been many attempts for social reader. We think that the actions of liking, commenting while browsing will also create great opportunities for people sharing the same interests to discover new and relevant content.

I helped developed the early concepts of the platform, explored its scope, and conducted design research. I made sketches, then mockups in multiple iterations, and then iteratively implemented the web front-end of the product. I have also explored identity design, planning execution and goto market strategies.

The part I like most about the design is the like/dislike bubble, it serves as a salient visualization of people’s collective attitudes, and its interactiveness also creates positive emotional response, and delightful experience of contributing.
