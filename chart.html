MUSIC CHART VISActive history of hottest artists on Billboard Top 10 in last decade.

In this data visualization project, we want to depict the careers of the most successful artists in the last decade: when they became popular, how long did their peaks last and when were the up-and-downs. The data is obtained from The Whitburn Project, which is a huge undertaking to preserve and share high-quality recordings of every popular song since the 1890s. Specifically we use the dataset called "Billboard Pop ME(1890-2012)".

Our initial design is inspired by Baby Name Voyager, with multi-line chart to depict and compare artists' roller coaster careers over time. However, after some exploratory visualization attempts, we realized we needed to focus on one specific part of the dataset, that is, the history of the hottest artists in the latest decade. In another round of brainstorming, we came up with a new visualization design, where Cartesian coordinates are replaced by polar coordinates. In this way, data can be represented in a more compact and attractive way. Each arc in the visualization shows the best chart performance of an artist in one year. The more saturated the color is, the better the performance. Clicking the arcs will trigger a detailed view to be shown, where the up-and-downs of one artist is depicted by line chart. To convey more information, mouseover the charts will reveal details of the track in question. The pictures of artists are retrieved via Last.fm API.

You can play with it here!

This project is for the interactive visualization assignment of CS448B Data Visualization at Stanford, completed by me and a good friend of mine, using D3.
