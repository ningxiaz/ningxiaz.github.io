A BIT MORE?

This is an age when everything is being redesigned. Technology tells us what we can do, while design tells us how we can do it right. My dream is to combine my interests and experiences in both fields to create products or systems that make our life more enjoyable and meaningful.

I was born and raised in a small town in centural China, where I produced quite a lof of little artworks and dreamed to be an artist, while later I realized I was better at maths and science. Upon entering college, Software Engineering happened to be my major and luckily I fell in love with creating things by programming.

During my undergraduate years at Fudan University in Shanghai, I was a PR assistant at Ogilvy, web developer at Schneider Electronics, summer researcher at University College Dublin, Ireland and chief developer of Innovation Lab at Standard Chartered Bank. In my third and fourth year, I assisted in TedxFDU and was actively involved in design and entrepreneurship events in Shanghai, such as design workshops held by IDEO and Frog Design and Barcamps by Techyizu.

Through those experiences, I realized with a strong technical background, an artistic mind, hunger for innovation and caring for humanities, there are ways I could make an impact. Thus, I decided to come to US and further my education in Human-Computer Interaction.

Now at Stanford, I'm honing my skills and enriching my experiences together with the smart innovators around.

My other passions include music, modern art, popular culture, fashion and travelling. I'm a big fan of indie music (The album wall above shows some of my collection) and have been to various live shows / major music festivals in Beijing, HK, Shanghai, Dublin and San Francisco.
