A BIT MORE?

I was born and raised in a small town in central China, where I created lots of little artworks and dreamed to be an artist. Later I shifted focus to maths and science, and majored in Software Engineering in college. Having realized that I'm interested in visual art, understanding people as well as science and enigneering, I decided to study Human-Computer Interaction, and pursue a career in design.

Thus I came to Stanford, after finishing my bachelor degree in Fudan University in Shanghai. My time at Stanford was overwhelmingly exciting and rewarding. One of my data visualization projects has been accepted as a poster by UIST 2013 and I attended the conference in St. Andrews, Scotland. I was on the CS team of Stanford’s Solar Decathlon project designing an integrated tablet app to guide homeowners develop sustainable lifestyle, where I explored combining data visualization with behavioral design. In the summer of 2013, I worked as a mobile design intern at Mobiata doing UI/UX design and product ideation. In my last year at Stanford, I TA'ed Andrew Ng's CS229 Machine Learning class, and took various classes in Art, AI and Social Science.

Now I work as the Director of User Experience Apropose, a Stanford startup that strives to bring data to the design process using novel technologies.

My other passions include indie music, modern art, fashion and travelling. I love going to live performance and art shows.
